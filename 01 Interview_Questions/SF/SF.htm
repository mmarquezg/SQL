    Classify Business Type
    Classify each business as either a restaurant, cafe, school, or other. A restaurant should have the word 'restaurant' in the business name. For cafes, either 'cafe', 'café', or 'coffee' can be in the business name. 'School' should be in the business name for schools. All other businesses should be classified as 'other'. Output the business name and the calculated classification.
    Table: sf_restaurant_health_violations

    SELECT distinct business_name,
    CASE
    WHEN business_name ilike any(array['%school%']) THEN 'school'
    WHEN lower(business_name) like any(array['%restaurant%']) THEN 'restaurant'
    WHEN lower(business_name) like any(array['%cafe%', '%café%', '%coffee%']) THEN 'cafe'
    ELSE 'other'
    END AS business_type
    FROM sf_restaurant_health_violations

    Team@StrataScratch
    2/16/2021, 6:57 PM

    Using array allows you to add multiple words in your statement. If you take a look at the 3rd statement you'll notice cafe and coffee being evaluated. The any() is required since it works like IN if you are evaluating multiple terms like cafe and coffee. But any() is more versatile because it allows for any operator rather than just =. Here we're using ilike and like operators. Here's more info about IN vs ANY (https://stackoverflow.com/questions/34627026/in-vs-any-operator-in-postgresql).

    As for your question on ilike and like, it's because we're fuzzy matching on those terms. When combined with the %, any business name that have other letters/words to the left or right of the term I'm searching for will still be found. I used a combination of ilike and like just to show that they can both be used.

    There are definitely multiple ways to approach this solution. I tried to show a complete albiet less simple approach. Check the "Solution from users" tab for other ways of solving this problem.

    The question "Classify Business Type" is not a problem in PostgreSQL, but the DISTINCT is case insensitive in MySQL currently. So a solution is this:

    SELECT
    DISTINCT(CAST(business_name AS CHAR CHARACTER SET utf8) COLLATE utf8_bin) AS business_name,
    CASE
        WHEN LOWER(business_name) REGEXP 'restaurant' THEN 'restaurant'
        WHEN LOWER(business_name) REGEXP 'cafe|café|coffee' THEN 'cafe'
        WHEN LOWER(business_name) REGEXP 'school' THEN 'school'
        ELSE 'other'
    END AS business_type
FROM sf_restaurant_health_violations;
